Please refine the current Freedom Square USA landing page based on the following design and conversion improvements.

Keep the overall structure and content, but make the site shorter, more modern, more readable, and less parchment-heavy.

## Main Goals

1. Make the page feel more hopeful, current, and practical while still patriotic and principled.
2. Reduce the overall page length by about 25–35%.
3. Improve color balance so it feels less like an old parchment document and more like a warm, serious civic community.
4. Improve web readability by reducing oversized section headings and shortening long sections.
5. Keep Apprentice as the only public membership offer.

## Color Updates

Use this revised palette:

* Deep Navy: `#101C35`
* Patriot Blue: `#1E3A5F`
* Warm White: `#FAF7F0`
* Soft Cream: `#F2E8D6`
* Muted Red: `#A53A2A`
* Deep Red Hover: `#872E23`
* Antique Gold: `#B8862F`
* Charcoal Text: `#253244`
* Soft Border: `#DDD2BF`

Use warm white as the main background. Use parchment/cream only in selected sections or cards. Use gold sparingly as an accent. Use muted red for primary CTA buttons. Use deep navy for contrast sections.

## Typography Updates

Keep the serif heading font, but reduce large section headings by 20–30%.

Hero H1 can stay large and dramatic.

Section headings should be smaller, easier to scan, and less overwhelming.

Avoid very long title-case headings when possible.

## Section Structure Changes

Shorten the page by combining sections.

Use this tighter section order:

1. Header
2. Hero
3. Problem / After the Event Ends
4. Freedom Requires More Than Concern
5. What Apprentice Members Receive
6. Learn From Trusted Constitutional Teachers
7. Is Freedom Square for You?
8. A Serious Community Requires a Serious Culture
9. Become an Apprentice Member
10. FAQ
11. Final CTA
12. Footer

Merge the current Problem Section and After Event Section into one section.

Merge the Better Way Section into the Freedom Requires section.

Merge the Apprentice Path section into the Apprentice Membership Offer section.

## Revised Problem Section

Replace the long heading with:

“ You Care About Your Country. But What Comes Next?”

Use shorter copy:

“National arguments feel endless. Social media rewards outrage. Serious conversations are often reduced to slogans, soundbites, and suspicion.

And even after an inspiring seminar, webinar, Constitution class, or civic event, the momentum can fade quickly.

Freedom Square USA gives that inspiration a year-round home — a place to keep learning, ask questions, build relationships, and take constructive next steps.”

Include the question cards:

* Where do I begin?
* Whom can I trust?
* What can I do that will actually make a meaningful difference?

## Freedom Requires Section

Make this a deep navy visual centerpiece.

Heading:

“Freedom Requires More Than Concern”

Include five cards or short emphasis blocks:

* Knowledge
* Character
* Responsibility
* Local Influence
* Good Company

Then include:

“Freedom Square USA helps citizens strengthen the principles, relationships, and habits needed to preserve freedom in real life — in families, churches, schools, neighborhoods, civic groups, businesses, and local communities.

This is not about passive consumption. It is about becoming the kind of citizen freedom requires.”

## Apprentice Offer Section

Combine the Apprentice Path and Offer sections.

Use this heading:

“Become an Apprentice Member”

Subheading:

“Learn the principles of liberty and strengthen your civic confidence.”

Include a short paragraph:

“Apprentice membership is for citizens who want to understand America’s founding documents more deeply, build principled relationships, and take constructive steps where they live.”

Include the price card with `$49/month`.

Include the CTA button.

Do not mention future membership tiers.

## Visual Rhythm

Add more contrast between sections.

Use deep navy for:

* Freedom Requires section
* Final CTA section
* optionally the Culture section

Avoid using cream/parchment for every section.

Use warm white as the main background.

Use left-aligned text in some middle sections so the page does not feel too formal or ceremonial.

## Header

Keep the header clean.

Make the CTA button slightly warmer using muted red instead of navy, or use navy only if red feels too strong.

## Mobile

Make sure mobile feels shorter and easier to scan.

Use:

* stacked cards
* full-width CTA buttons
* FAQ accordion
* shorter section headings
* sticky bottom CTA after scrolling past the hero

## Preserve This Core Line

Keep this line prominent:

“This is a community for citizens who are tired of the noise, yet still willing to do what freedom requires.”

Use it in the hero or final CTA, but do not overuse it.

## Final Goal

The finished page should feel like a serious, hopeful, principled civic community — not a political campaign, not an outrage site, and not an old historical brochure.
